# Exercise 3 – Communicating Data Insights

## Data Story

This website presents a data story about television energy consumption.  
The target audience is consumers who are planning to buy a new TV in the Australian market.

The purpose of the story is to help consumers understand how screen size and screen technology can affect electricity use. This is important because energy consumption can influence long-term electricity costs.

The key questions explored are:

- What TV sizes are most common?
- Do larger TVs use more energy?
- Does screen technology affect energy consumption?

The main finding is that larger TVs generally consume more energy. Screen technology may also affect energy use, so consumers should compare both size and technology before buying a TV.

## About the Data

### Data Source

The dataset used in this project is the TV energy consumption dataset provided for the course exercises.

### Data Processing

The data was explored using KNIME.  
Charts from Exercise 2 were reused and presented as part of the Week 3 data story.

The visualisations used include:

- a histogram showing the distribution of TV sizes
- a scatter plot showing screen size compared with energy consumption
- a bar chart comparing energy use by TV size category
- a grouped bar chart comparing screen technology and energy use

Screen size was converted from centimetres to inches to make the information easier for consumers to understand. TVs were also grouped into small, medium, and large categories to make comparisons clearer.

### Privacy

The dataset does not contain personal or sensitive information.  
It focuses on television product information, so the privacy risk is low.

### Accuracy and Limitations

The dataset may not include every TV available in the Australian market.  
Some information may also be incomplete or outdated.

The findings should be understood as patterns from this dataset, not as a complete summary of all televisions.

### Ethics

The visualisations are intended to present the data clearly and fairly.  
Care was taken not to exaggerate the findings or make misleading claims.

The goal is to help consumers make more informed choices, not to promote or criticise any specific brand.
